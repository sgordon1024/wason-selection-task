About this task: The Wason Selection Task was devised by Peter Wason in 1966. Most people struggle with the abstract version — typically only 10–25% get it right. In 1989, Leda Cosmides showed that performance jumps dramatically when the same logic is framed as a social contract, suggesting humans have a specialized "cheater-detection" system that evolved before general abstract reasoning.
